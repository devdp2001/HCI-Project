High-Fidelity Prototype




Prototype 1

As a user, I want to immediately see the calendar when I get to the ELC homepage, so the calendar is readily visible.

As a user, I want to see “Upcoming Events” below the calendar view, so that I can see which assignments I have due in the future.

As a user, I want the days on the calendar view to have a some sort of visible designation to denote which days have items due/ events, so I can easily find dates that have something due for any class.


- the calendar is placed above the fold for instant visibility for the user (1b)
-The calendar features colored dots below the dates to signify which days have x type(s) of assignment due (3a)
-the calendar shows upcoming events in a drop-down section below the days of the month, so students can see exactly what assignments are due soon (2a)

Testing Protocol


1. What is your research question? It should be simple, falsifiable, and related to the effectiveness of your design in solving your problem of study.
Can ELC’s calendar be improved by placing it above the fold and incorporating color?

What methodology will you use for testing and why?
To ensure accurate testing results, we want to use two methodologies for testing.

Our first method is practical, simple, and useful for testing the user experience of existing eLC users. For a fixed period of time until we are satisfied with the results, we will include a 1 question survey near the top of eLC's homepage. When a user logs in, they will be able to provide their answer to our question "do you like the recent changes to eLC's homepage and calendar?" Yes or no answer buttons will be provided but users will not be forced to participate. They will also be able to close the survey popup window without providing an answer.

Our second method will observe the impact of our changes through two experimental groups consisting of random high school seniors or recent high school graduates (participants that have never used eLC before). Students in both groups will be asked to navigate through eLC to find course information using the calendar and other UI. Group 1 will use the current version of eLC and Group 2 will use our new prototype of eLC. After completing the assigned task, both groups will complete a short survey to rate their experience, ease of use, and task completion satisfaction. Procedure (2 sample T-Test):

- Take a random sample of 100 students (p1)
  - Each student uses old elc calendar for at least 30 seconds when they use elc
  - Students rate the calendar 1 or 0 (satisfactory = 1, unsatisfactory = 0)
- Take another random sample of 100 students (p2)
  - Each student uses new elc calendar for at least 30 seconds when they use elc
  - Students rate the calendar 1 or 0 (satisfactory = 1, unsatisfactory = 0)

- Hypotheses
  - Null: p1 - p2 = 0
  - Alternative: p1 - p2 < 0
  - P1 = tally of satisfactory / total students in p1
  - P2 = tally of satisfactory / total students in p2

- Calculate the test statistic and find the p-value. Compare p-value against an alpha of 0.05.
  - We expect the p-value to be less than 0.05, which will reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the new UI is better.
